# Dynatrace Customer Solutions Organization Blueprint (v6)

## Mission
To make **AI-driven solution creation** a first-class capability across Dynatrace—turning every customer engagement into reusable, governed, and enterprise-grade outcomes.  
The Customer Solutions organization connects innovation, governance, and execution under one unified structure.

---

## Organizational Inputs and Alignment

### Key Inputs
1. **CCO PulseBoard** – A continuously updating signal system reflecting real-time customer sentiment, needs, and opportunities across all strategic accounts.
   - Data sources: email, Slack, Salesforce, support, GitLab, and customer engagement.
   - Managed by AI Engineering and Solution Architects to surface key solution needs and platform gaps.

2. **Solutions Board** – The centralized prioritization mechanism for all Customer Solutions activities.
   - Managed by **Solutions Delivery**.
   - Inputs originate from the **Chief Customer Officer's Senior Leadership Team (CCO SLT)**, **Corporate Dynatrace Leadership Team (DLT)**, and **CCO PulseBoard** signals.
   - Defines quarterly and annual priorities, aligning Solution Architects, Solutions Development, and AI Engineering.

### Corporate and Product Alignment
The Customer Solutions organization acts as a **bridge between Dynatrace corporate strategy, the product organization, and the field**.  
- **D1 Alignment:** Continuous feedback loop with Dynatrace’s product teams to ensure customer-led insights directly influence product roadmaps.  
- **Corporate Alignment:** Partnership with the CCO SLT and Corporate DLT ensures business outcomes translate into prioritized solution initiatives.  
- **Product Collaboration:** Ensures all delivered solutions adhere to product architecture, scalability, and roadmap alignment standards.

---

## Cross-Team Solution Initiative Model
Large-scale or strategic solution initiatives will always be **led by a Customer-Facing Solution Architect**.  
Each initiative includes **one embedded representative from each of the other three teams**:  

- **AI Engineering** – Provides governance, AI enablement, and LangGraph integration.  
- **Solutions Development** – Ensures engineering discipline, scalability, and readiness for delivery.  
- **Solutions Delivery** – Drives prioritization, communication, and adoption strategy.  

This ensures end-to-end continuity from **concept → build → productization → delivery**, while maintaining shared accountability and quality across all four teams.

## Solution Architecture Organization

### Overview
The Solution Architecture Organization represents the **front-line technical leadership** of the Customer Solutions group.  
They are responsible for **owning the customer’s technical success**, driving solution design, and closing the translation gap between customer needs, product capabilities, and internal delivery.  

All Architects are both **builders and strategists**, leveraging AI-powered Forge environments and governed Claude skills to deliver enterprise-grade outcomes faster than ever before.

---

### Role Hierarchy and Responsibilities

| Role | Coverage Ratio | Account Size (ACV) | Key Responsibilities |
|------|----------------|--------------------|----------------------|
| **Director / Managing Architect** | Variable | Strategic / Multi-region | • Executive engagement and critical account architecture<br>• Internal design authority for cross-customer or platform-wide solutions<br>• Mentorship and guidance for all Architects<br>• Bridge between Product, Corporate, and Customer domain<br>• Ensure translation between customer gaps and internal design decisions |
| **Principal Solution Architect** | 2:1 | ≥ $3M ACV | • Technical authority for highest-value accounts<br>• Leads multi-solution or platform-scale efforts<br>• Defines end-to-end customer observability architecture<br>• Partners with AI Engineering and Solutions Delivery on critical design and governance |
| **Senior Solution Architect** | 3:1 | ≥ $2M ACV | • Account-level solution ownership<br>• Drives expansion and adoption initiatives<br>• Identifies cross-account solution opportunities<br>• Co-develops reusable patterns with Solutions Development |

> All Architects align under the central Architect Team and act as the **technical authority within each Integrated Account Team**, owning the customer’s observability success and solution outcomes.

### Architect Projects and Claude Enablement
Every Solution Architect maintains an **Architect Project** per assigned account—a dedicated workspace that contains:  
- **Customer-specific solution assets** (Dashboards, Workflows, Monaco templates, Extensions).  
- **Documentation repositories** generated through Claude agents and LangGraph automation.  
- **Diagram-as-Code systems** powered by Claude for architecture visualizations and dependency maps.  
- **AI Engineering ingestion hooks** so that account-level insights continuously feed into global solution models.  

Each Architect Project includes:  
- **Predefined Claude skill sets** for DQL, automation, design, and documentation.  
- **Blueprint generators** for proposals and internal collaboration.  
- **Governance policies** ensuring IP, security, and compliance standards are met by design.

---

### Solution Pattern Initiation and Architectural Custodianship
Solution Architects are the **originators and curators of solution patterns** across the Customer Solutions organization.  
They identify emerging needs, validate technical feasibility, and ensure the architectural integrity of every solution developed within their customer spheres.

**Initiation and Proposal**
- Solution Architects frequently **initiate new solution patterns** based on recurring customer needs, platform gaps, or innovation opportunities surfaced through the **CCO PulseBoard** and **Solutions Board**.  
- They may **propose net-new solutions** or **guide the evolution of existing solutions**, shaping foundational architecture and delivery design from inception through release.  
- Every proposal is documented through **Architect Projects** and routed through **Solutions Development** and **Solutions Delivery** for formal productization and scale.

**Ongoing Architectural Stewardship**
- Even after a solution enters development or delivery, Solution Architects **remain engaged**—providing **design guidance, validation, and refinement** based on live customer outcomes.  
- Architecture as a whole stays actively connected to **every solution impacting its customer base**, ensuring design evolution stays aligned with real-world needs.  
- This model closes the gap between **customer experience and internal engineering**, ensuring all solutions—whether built by Architects, Development, or Product—retain deep field alignment.

**Core Principles**
- **Architect-Led Innovation:** Architects are empowered to lead solution design, not just contribute to it.  
- **Lifecycle Accountability:** Architectural involvement persists from inception to delivery to continuous improvement.  
- **Customer Reality Alignment:** Every solution must reflect the challenges, context, and feedback from customer-facing practitioners.

### Tactical Solutions Architects (On-Demand Support and Acceleration)
In addition to account-aligned Solution Architects, a specialized **Tactical Solutions Architecture group** operates globally to provide **on-demand, high-velocity technical support** for critical customer or field needs.

**Purpose**
- Deliver rapid-response technical assistance for complex or urgent challenges.  
- Build proofs of concept or deploy short-horizon solutions outside fixed account allocations.  
- Support field teams, Services, Support, or Delivery on emerging customer opportunities or escalations.  

**Request Intake**
- Tactical requests originate through:
  - **CCO PulseBoard escalations** (emerging customer issues).  
  - **Solutions Delivery** (field or CCO SLT-directed requests).  
  - **Support and Services** (critical incidents needing architect-level expertise).  

**Operating Model**
- Queue-based, priority-driven task allocation.  
- Works alongside assigned account Architects to ensure consistency and context.  
- Every engagement produces an **Architect Project artifact**—documenting code, context, and learnings—which feeds back into Solutions Development for future reuse.

**Output**
- Rapid, documented solutions; reusable assets; and AI-captured artifacts that improve future responsiveness.

## AI-First Enablement and Governance Execution
The Customer Solutions organization embraces a new era where **AI systems replace traditional, static enablement and best-practice models**.  
Gone are the days of building long-form presentations or manually maintained documentation that quickly becomes obsolete or inconsistently followed across Services, Support, Partners, and Customers.

**Dynamic Knowledge through LangGraph**
- All best-practice guidance, implementation frameworks, and architectural reference material are **curated, versioned, and delivered via LangGraph pipelines**.
- LangGraph connects live inputs from **Architect Projects**, **CCO PulseBoard**, and **Solutions Board**, producing continuously evolving solution intelligence accessible by every stakeholder.

**Governance as an Executable Framework**
- Governance no longer lives in policy documents—it is **embedded directly into AI agents, Claude skills, and plug-ins**.  
- These agents enforce architectural, security, and compliance standards automatically at build and deployment time.  
- The **Claude Skill and Plug-in Marketplace** enables teams to extend or specialize governance logic without duplicating or rewriting guidance.

**Benefits**
- **Continuous Accuracy:** All enablement and governance materials update in real time as solutions evolve.  
- **Scalable Consistency:** Every consultant, partner, and customer operates within the same living governance system.  
- **Reduced Maintenance Overhead:** AI agents handle updates, traceability, and policy versioning automatically.  
- **Executable Knowledge:** Instead of reading guidance, practitioners run it—via Claude-powered automation and LangGraph workflows.

**Outcome**
> **Governance becomes code, enablement becomes intelligent, and best practices become living systems.**

## Governance and Branching Workflow
1. **Discovery → Build (Solution Architect):** Construct solution in a Forge environment, branch from `main` → `feature/*`.  
2. **Submission (SA → Solutions Development):** Submit pull request with technical brief and validation tests.  
3. **Gate Checks (Solutions Dev + AI Eng):** Automated compliance (SBOM, SAST/DAST, license, PII, performance).  
4. **Refactor and Harden (Solutions Dev):** Align with engineering and governance standards.  
5. **Release Train (Solutions Dev + Delivery):** Tag release, publish artifacts, update roadmap.  
6. **Enable and Adopt (Solutions Delivery):** Publish enablement assets, field communications, and adoption metrics.  
7. **Feedback Loop:** CCO PulseBoard + field data → Solutions Board → adjusted priorities.

**Note:**  
Any solution built by a Solution Architect that is intended for broader release must be validated and productized by **Solutions Development** and **Solutions Delivery** before publication or reuse across accounts.

## Summary
The Customer Solutions organization aligns four specialized teams—**Solution Architecture**, **AI Engineering**, **Solutions Development**, and **Solutions Delivery**—driven by **CCO PulseBoard** signals and **Solutions Board** priorities.  
Within Solution Architecture, both **strategic relationship architects** and **tactical architects** collaborate globally to identify, initiate, and guide solutions throughout their lifecycle.  
Through LangGraph, Claude Agents, and AI-executable governance, Dynatrace transforms traditional architecture and enablement into **living, intelligent, and continuously governed systems**—ensuring it remains the global leader in **AI-driven observability innovation**.
